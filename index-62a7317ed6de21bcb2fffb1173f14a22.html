About Building Bridges PGH

In the wake of a recent local hate crime, a group of concerned Pittsburghers came together to discuss ways to show solidarity with those in the region who are under threat from bigotry.

Building Bridges PGH is the result of that conversation.

In discussing options for action, it quickly became clear that a sign campaign would be a concrete way for us to demonstrate our support and make all of our neighbors feel more welcome.

As Pittsburgh is “The City of Bridges,” it was agreed that the symbol of the bridge would communicate our commitment to connection and inclusion. A local designer, Hilary Schenker of Green Comma Media, lent her talents to creating a sign that delivers an anti-hate message in the city’s own distinctive black and gold. The message, translations, and campaign as a whole represent the efforts of many in our community who are committed to a diverse and united Pittsburgh.
